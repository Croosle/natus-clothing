About

Destiny. A powerful force that pre-determines the course of events. Destiny. The force that pre-determined the creation of NATUS. Around the end of 2017, the aftermath of a business failure triggered a desire to create a new clothing label. A clothing label that would balance both the respect for a clean aesthetic and the drive to design couture-esque, experimental garments. Our first collection, FALLEN LEGENDS//01, was the result of a perfect equilibrium we found. Inspired by streetwear design, and executed with high fashion precision, the combination produced t-shirts worthy of haute-couture status. Made locally in Scotland, every piece is made to order, using only the finest materials and most, meticulous processes. On reflection, if it wasn’t for failure, there would have been no reason to establish NATUS. The previous company was ultimately destined to fail. Destined to fail so that NATUS could be born out of it. If you’re wondering what NATUS means, it’s Latin for ‘destined by nature’. If you’re wondering how to pronounce it, there is no right or wrong way as long as you remember who we are. 1 /HUNDRED POLICY To ensure every piece we make to order is a truly one off, each garment is numbered. and we've capped the max. quantity we sell to just 100 pcs, per design. Size Guide

  •  Oversized shape. Fits wide and slightly longline.
  •  Double layer design. Using a specially developed technique, we’ve taken two separately made t-shirts and combined both pieces seamlessly. The construction allows a second layer, on the inside, to hang freely while remaining securely in place.
  •  The sleeves are cut extra large, and designed so that the layer under becomes visible if rolled up.

GUEVARA

180

Size Guide

  •  Oversized shape. Fits wide and slightly longline.
  •  Double layer design. Using a specially developed technique, we’ve taken two separately made t-shirts and combined both pieces seamlessly. The construction allows a second layer, on the inside, to hang freely while remaining securely in place.
  •  The sleeves are cut extra large, and designed so that the layer under becomes visible if rolled up.
  •  On top is a soft, sandy brown cotton jersey.
  •  The layer below is a light jersey fabric. The material is printed, using graphics to reproduce the effect of digital, desert camouflage.
  •  On the chest sits an embroidery piece, an homage to Che Guevara - his legendary portrait . Using at least 10 different coloured threads, the flat stitching comes together to create an illusive 3D pixelated effect. And unlike any other embroidery, our shape is broken down into individual squares which are stitched and coloured one at a time in perfect uniformity.

Description - Inspired by Che Guevara’s ‘Motorcycle Diaries’:


Dear Diary, It was a morning in October. The early dawn light shone through the curtain and encouraged me to get up. I felt a desire to create, to build something. I looked for fabrics to work with and in a pile of scraps, two stood out. One was a soft, sandy brown cotton jersey. The other was polar opposite. It was a digital desert camouflage print. Both fabrics were light, and despite their contrast, I wanted them to become as one. Halfway through my morning coffee, I pictured a layered outfit, and its appeal. Two different tops, one on top of the other, working in tandem. What if I could recreate that? Without the need for two separate pieces…I didn’t have a lot of the print fabric so I thought, that could be the base, and the sand fabric could be the t-shirt on top. So if you looked at it from the front or back you’d see, a standard t-shirt on top, and part of a patterned t-shirt underneath. It’d even work similarly for the sleeves, if you rolled them up. First, I had to map out the shape of the top. I looked around for inspiration, and found nothing. I realised what I was missing, a cosy t-shirt to feel wrapped up in. So I decided, it’d have to be oversized. Not so much, long, because I’m not into those dress length t-shirts. But wide, like one of those stretched out jumpers everyone has at the bottom of their wardrobe. As I finished sewing the two fabrics together, I realised it was getting dark and I hadn’t taken a break since I woke up. Problem was, I couldn’t stop thinking about what would complete the piece. I had this crazy idea of making embroidery, but had no design or idea in mind. I was about to go to bed when I saw it, staring at me. The poster above my desk of the iconic Che Guevara silhouette. I couldn’t sleep after that, so I stayed up all night, creating a pixelated version of the famous portrait. It was embroidery, literally made from individual squares that looked like pixels, on a piece of white denim that I sewed on to the left breast pocket area of the top t-shirt. I thought it looked like a playing card, because I sewed on the letter ‘G’ and a mini version of the main shape in each corner, like the ace of spades. The whole top took me a full 24 hours to make, but it was worth it. P.S If, somehow, you've came across my diary and you’re reading this entry, it may be hard to imagine what I’m describing. Which is why, I’ve included a photo to your right, enjoy. :)

Size Guide

  •  Oversized shape. Fits wide and slightly longline.

  •  The sleeves are cut extra large, and designed so that the layer under becomes visible if rolled up.
  •  On top is a soft, pastel blue cotton jersey.
  •  The layer below is a light, textured crepe jersey. The material is printed, using graphics to reproduce the effect of a watercolour painting.
  •  On the chest sits an embroidery piece, an homage to Alexander McQueen - the iconic skull symbol. Using at least 10 different coloured threads, the flat stitching comes together to create an illusive 3D pixelated effect. And unlike any other embroidery, our shape is broken down into individual squares which are stitched and coloured one at a time in perfect uniformity.

Description - Inspired by the way Isabella Blow spoke about Alexander McQueen:


It’s Isabella Blow speaking from London on behalf of McQueen for NATUS. Isabella Blow. B.L.O.W.

When I first saw McQueen, it was the colours and silhouette which initially drew me to it. It was something very special. Maybe it’s because, I’ve always had blue wherever I go. My apartment in New York was light blue. This particular blue reminds me of skies in winter - that cold pastel blue. And that hint of yellow in the print, is like the faint glow of the sun, dipping below the tree-line. It was vivid, extreme.

And the way it moved, it moved in a way I’ve never seen for a t-shirt. It wasn’t overly long, but it was wide. Like a monolith. As were the sleeves - they had this very oversized silhouette, very chic.

People think I like really funky stuff. I like classic with a twist. I like things that are cut beautifully. I’m obsessed with the idea of two layers in one piece. There’s this mixture of smooth, from the soft cotton jersey on top, and texture, from the light crepe fabric below. It’s erotic. The combination of contrasting materials works, you know….there is something very kind of naughty about it.

Can you feel the emotion in the print? It’s about love. Love for colour, love for adventure. An artist, using his watercolours, to splash, smear, streak, give life to his canvas. Blending blues, yellows, greys, to create this incredible beauty. It's elegant, yet thrilling. Yeah it’s just pure luxury, very sexy. And if you like that burst of colour on your sleeves, roll them up - show some of that wonderful print underneath.

Oh darling, I haven’t even told you about the embroidery. It's complex and modern, yet classical. I sense the skull has a deeper meaning, not representative of death but of foundation. It's the essential structure that holds our most intricate organ, the brain. Each block of embroidered thread is singular and links with one another to create a whole shape, much like the way pixels on an old computer display would make up an image. The variation in every pixel’s colour gives it such an individual feel. Its effect is very severe. Your own mega-sized ace of skulls.

I hate crap, I like craftsmanship, and this is skill in its purest form. Once your head goes over that t-shirt, it’s the most comfortable thing in the world. It’s no longer a t-shirt, it’s like wearing a piece of couture.
